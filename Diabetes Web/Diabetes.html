Understanding Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic health condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. When you have diabetes, your body either doesn't make enough insulin or can't use the insulin it makes effectively.

Types of Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

An autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little to no insulin. Typically diagnosed in children and young adults.

Type 2 Diabetes

The most common form of diabetes. Your body doesn't use insulin well and can't maintain normal blood sugar levels.

Gestational Diabetes

Develops in some women during pregnancy and usually goes away after giving birth.

Common Symptoms

  • Increased thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Blurred vision
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Slow-healing wounds

Prevention and Management

Lifestyle Changes

Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, manage stress, and get regular check-ups.
